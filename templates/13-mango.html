MANGO


Mango is a popular fruit crop in India, known for its sweet and succulent taste. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The major mango-growing regions in India include Maharashtra, Uttar Pradesh, Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka, and Gujarat. Mango trees require well-drained soil, regular irrigation, and adequate sunlight. The flowering and fruiting season varies depending on the mango variety. Proper pruning, pest control, and fertilization are essential for optimal growth and high-quality yield. Mangoes are typically harvested during the summer months, and India is one of the largest producers of mangoes in the world.

Summary


Type of crop : KHARIF

Ideal pH : 5.5-6.5

Ideal Soil type : LOAM SOILS

Ideal Relative Humidity : 90% or Higher

Ideal Temperature(in °C) : 21-24°C

Ideal Rainfall(in mm) : 1000-1250mm throughout the growing season

Ideal Sowing Time : January-February
