MUNGBEAN


Moongbeans, also known as mung beans or green gram, are widely cultivated in India. They prefer warm and subtropical climates with temperatures between 25°C and 35°C (77°F and 95°F). Moongbeans thrive in well-drained loamy soil with a pH ranging from 6.0 to 7.5. They have moderate water requirements and can withstand drought conditions to some extent.

Regular weeding and proper irrigation management are crucial for their growth. Moongbeans are susceptible to pests like aphids, whiteflies, and thrips, requiring appropriate pest control measures. Harvesting is done when the pods turn yellow or brown, and the beans are fully mature. Moongbeans are highly nutritious and widely used in Indian cuisine and for sprouting purposes.

Summary


Type of crop : KHARIF

Ideal pH : 5.5-8.5

Ideal Soil type : Loamy to Sandy Loam

Ideal Relative Humidity : 90% or Higher

Ideal Temperature(in °C) : 25°C-35°C

Ideal Rainfall(in mm) : 60-90 cm

Ideal Sowing Time : March-April
