MOTHBEANS


Moth beans, also known as matki or Turkish gram, are small, drought-tolerant legumes that belong to the Fabaceae family. Moth beans are known for their nutritional richness and versatility in culinary applications. They are a good source of protein, dietary fiber, vitamins, and minerals, making them a healthy addition to a balanced diet. In terms of appearance, moth beans are small, oval-shaped, and light brown in color with a distinct nutty flavor.

These beans are also used as a filling in savory snacks and are often ground into flour for making traditional flatbreads. With their nutritional benefits and culinary versatility, moth beans continue to be cherished and enjoyed by people around the world.

Summary


Type of crop : RABI

Ideal pH : 6.0-7.5

Ideal Soil type : LOAM SOILS

Ideal Relative Humidity : 70%-80%

Ideal Temperature(in °C) : 25°C-35°C

Ideal Rainfall(in mm) : 300-500mm throughout the growing season

Ideal Sowing Time : May-June
